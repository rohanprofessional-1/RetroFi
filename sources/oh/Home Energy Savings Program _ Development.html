Home Energy Savings Program

April 25, 2024 | Development

Through the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA), the State of Ohio was awarded $249 million by the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), via a formula grant, to help eligible Ohioans save on home energy costs by offering rebates towards projects aimed at improving energy efficiency through the following programs:

  • High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate Act (HEEHRA) - $124,150,970
  • Home Efficiency Rebates (HOMES) - $124,628,229

The Ohio Department of Development’s Office of Energy and Environment is currently awaiting approval from DOE on the program application, which was submitted Nov. 12, 2024.  

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will applications for both HEEHRA and HOMES be available online?  

A: We anticipate both online and in paper form. 

 

Q: How long do you anticipate the approval process to take?  

A: The U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) has told states the anticipated program approval process should last no longer than 60 days from application submission. 

 

Q: What certifications will be required to be considered a qualified contractor to assess the energy savings potential?  

A: Certifications and requirements will include but not be limited to industry accepted home performance industry credentials, training requirements, business insurance and licensure, skills standards, and labor standards. We will provide updates as this portion of the program design is further defined. 

 

Q: Will there be publicly available lists of trained/qualified contractors?  

A: All program information will be available on this webpage once the program has been approved by DOE.  

 

Q: As a contractor, will you provide information to help me inform my clients about this program?  

A: We're developing a contractor engagement strategy and will communicate with contractor networks over the next few months. 

 

Q: Will these rebates apply to the cost of installation as well as materials?  

A: Yes. 

 

Q: Will there be production goals, and how will those be determined?  

A: Yes. These goals will be refined throughout the planning process and based upon the final DOE-approved program. 

 

Q: Is this program administered at the state level, or by local community action agency or other entities? 

A: The Department of Development will be administering this program, in partnership with DOE. 

 

Q: How much of the grant funds can be used for administrative purposes?  

A: DOE provides that up to 20% of program funds can be used for administrative expenses. 

 

Q: Are churches eligible?  

A: No. DOE has specifically designed this program for single- and multi-family homes only. This program is not available to general nonprofits or non-residential units. 

 

Q: Will specific information about income (gross, AGI, SS benefits, etc.) for single member households be on the website?  

A: This webpage will provide tools and resources regarding program eligibility requirements. 

 

Q: Does your income disqualify you if it is too high?  

A: Two distinct programs are being offered. The Home Electrification and Appliance Rebate Program (HOMES) is intended for all Ohioans, regardless of income. The Home Efficiency Rebate Program (HEEHRA) is an income-qualified program and is accessible to individuals earning less than 150% of the Area Median Income (AMI). 

 

Q: Where can we find the formula used for our area's AMI?  

A: The following webpage contains a helpful tool to search for AMI by county: https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/il.html 

 

Q: For income verification, is the intent for an interested household to get their income verified by the Department of Development (and/or selected third-party vendors) prior to selecting a contractor/installer to do the home energy upgrade work?  

A: Yes. 

 

Q: What website can we check at for income? How do I find out if I am eligible?  

A: Prior to program launch, our intent is to create a program specific website and online application portal. Eligibility criteria will be provided closer to the program launch, including program income limits. 

 

Q: Are renters eligible for the program?  

A: Yes. 

 

Q: How can I provide feedback if I can't attend a virtual or in-person event?  

A: Complete our Feedback Survey here. The survey is now closed.

 

Q: Can I receive a copy of the PowerPoint presentation from the feedback sessions?  

A: The PowerPoint presentation can be found under the "Resources" section on this webpage.

 

Q: Where do I find a list of J40 communities by county?  

A: You can locate the J40 communities by using the interactive CEJST map tool here: https://screeningtool.geoplatform.gov/en/#3/33.47/-97.5 

 

Q: For planning purposes, does a change in federal administration as a result of the presidential election change the Ohio state team's timeline/plans for rolling out this program?  

A: Ohio will respond to DOE guidance in this circumstance.  

 

Q: How do I sign up for updates?  

A: This webpage will be updated regularly with information and additional FAQs as the program's development progresses.

 

Q: Are there any plans to implement a time frame in which landlords who do take advantage of the rebates can't raise the rent for their tenants afterwards?  

A: The Home Energy Savings Program's two-year rental affordability requirement will help ensure investments intended to serve low-income housing directly benefit this group and that upgrades do not lead to unaffordable housing. The State of Ohio will align landlord participation requirements with DOE guidance.  

 

Q: When will rebates be available?  

A: The Department of Development anticipates launching the program in the fourth quarter of 2024 or the first quarter of 2025, depending upon when the State of Ohio receives program approval and release of funds from DOE. 

 

Q: Will contractor training be available for the program?  

A: The State of Ohio has requested contractor training grant funds and plans to deploy those in support of this program. 

 

Q: Will there be contractors/energy auditors that can help educate people who have an emergency replacement that is eligible to be replaced through the program?  

A: The applicant will be able to apply for the program under any circumstance and if eligible will receive information from trained and approved energy auditors and contractors. 

 

Q: Does the contractor take the amount off the invoice and then get paid from Egis or the State of Ohio?  

A: We are developing the program at this time and are still working on final details.  

 

Q: Are the energy audits ASHRAE level II or III?  

A: As per DOE guidelines, multi-family audits are ASHRAE level II compliant. 

 

Q: Who performs the energy audits? Are they free?  

A: We are developing a holistic program process. Energy audits will be provided by the program at no cost to applicants. 

 

Q: Is there a funding cap per unit?  

A: There are individual funding caps based on multiple variables for both programs. 

 

Q: Do you have to be the tenant to apply for the program or can landlord also apply?  

A: Either the tenant or the landlord can apply for the program and our intent will be to have an approval process which will have to be agreed upon by the owner/landlord of the property. 

 

Q: For the multi-family program how is income eligibility determined?  

A: Income eligibility is based off of AMI as set forth in the DOE guidelines. 

 

Q: Can a homeowner receive improvements from both HOMES and HEERA?  

A: Yes, if an applicant qualifies for both programs, they may receive benefits from each program. 

 

Q: What is the minimum percentage for energy savings to be eligible for the program?  

A: The minimum percentage to be eligible for the HOMES program is 20%. There is no minimum for HEEHRA. 

 

Q: Does the program allow for upgrades in excess of what the homeowner is eligible for?  

A: No. 

 

Q: Will upgraded HVAC systems require a Manual J analysis to ensure correct sizing with Energy Star tolerances?  

A: DOE guidelines will not require a Manual J analysis for the sizing of HVAC equipment. Sizing of HVAC equipment will be determined and verified by a qualified HVAC contractor. 

 

Q: Does the HVAC contractor need to be trained to participate in the program?  

A: We anticipate training will be provided for the HOMES and HEEHRA programs. 

 

Q: If an energy auditor finds the need for work outside the skill set of a single contractor, will multiple contractors be used?  

A: Multiple contractors will be used in support of the program, potentially depending on the specific need. 

 

Q: Will case managers be partnered with specific energy auditors and contractors, or be assigned to specific geographic regions?

A: We are developing the program at this time and are still working on final details.  

 

Q: If something with regards to the program changes midway through implementation, how will changes to the rebate program be rolled out? What date (application date, audit date, install date, etc.) will drive what version of the program a home will fall under?  

A: The State of Ohio will work closely with DOE staff if something like this occurs after program implementation. 

 

Q: How will energy savings for each upgraded home be determined? 

A: Per DOE guidelines, this is determined through the process of the energy audit. 

 

Q: Has Ohio applied for the program?  

A: Ohio has applied for the early administrative funds and is on track to apply for the full program in the fall of 2024. 
 

Q: Will there be opportunities for non-profits and other agencies to collaborate on outreach for the program.  

A: The State of Ohio intends to collaborate with agencies throughout the planning process to determine the most effective program implementation plan. 


Q: How will this program be partnered/blended with HWAP programs in the state? 

A: The State of Ohio is currently collaborating with HWAP providers to identify opportunities for program integration. 


Q: Has there been any discussion with state agencies about pairing these funds with low-income housing tax credits?  

A: The State of Ohio is in discussion with other state agencies for potential braiding opportunities. 


Q: Will homeowners required to do an energy audit if they already know the equipment they want to install (i.e., heat pump)? 

A: Per DOE guidance, an energy audit is required to verify savings targets set forth by federal regulations. 

 

Q: Will energy audits be required for HEEHRA? Shouldn't they be necessary for electrification goals as long as existing appliance fuel sources are verified?  

A: Per DOE guidance, HEEHRA projects must be qualified electrification projects (QEP) and therefore verification will be required. 

 

Q: If community partners are already doing energy audits, will those qualify? 

A: Per program guidelines, energy audits must be compliant with DOE guidance. 

 

Q: What work is being done to provide information on some of the lesser-known technologies like heat pumps?  

A: Our office will be releasing educational materials regarding eligible improvements. 

 

Q: How will you deal with properties that don’t meet local code and should be designated as uninhabitable? 

A: If the property cannot be made safe, sanitary, and secure it becomes ineligible for this program. 

 

Q: With multi-family, does the work/install need to happen all at once or can it be phased? 

A: Yes, the work can be phased depending on the property needs and contractor schedule. 

 

Q: Will program funding support fuel switching for residents who are interested in switching from gas to electric? 

A: The program offers the opportunity for electrification through qualified electrification projects (QEP). 

 

Q: Can an eligible household with a non-working furnace have a working unit installed? 

A: The eligible household must reach the energy savings threshold to participate in the HOMES program. 

 

Q: In working toward bill neutrality, will there be a tailored calculator for individual households to estimate bill impacts using their energy data? 

A: Estimated impacts will be provided through the energy audit for eligible homes. 

 

Q: Will the rebates for the HEEHRA program be point-of-sale rebates?  

A: The State of Ohio is currently reviewing the ways that the program rebates will be delivered, and this will be decided during implementation blueprint planning. 

 

Q: What is a qualified contractor? 

A: The State of Ohio is currently still in the process of designing the program, however, in the future the state will post contractor guidelines and requirements. 

 

Q: Who will verify program eligibility? Can this be contracted to an existing non-profit?  

A: The State of Ohio will be handling eligibility verification; however, non-profits may be used to confirm categorical eligibility. 

 

Q: Will those with categorical eligibility be automatically notified? 

A: No, there will be no automatic eligibility notifications for the programs. 

 

Q: How will the old appliances be disposed of? 

A: A white goods management plan will be agreed upon during the implementation stage. 

 

Q: How does this work in conjunction with IRS tax credits or is that what this is? 

A: This is not an IRS tax credit program. For information regarding specific tax credits, please contact the IRS. 

 

Q: Will the State be advocating for more energy efficient appliances for manufactured homes? 

A: Manufactured homes are eligible for the program. 

 

Q: Are solar panels eligible? 

A: No, solar panels are not eligible for this program, however, there are other programs to assist with that type of improvement. 

 

Q: What year of AMI will be used?  

A: We are currently using the year based off of the HUD AMI calculator. 

 

Q: Is AMI based on a four-person household, or do you adjust by the number of people in your household? 

A: The AMI will be calculated based upon household income and household size. 

 

Q: Is AMI at the county level or is this narrower? 

A: HUD uses Census Tracts data and calculates AMI by county or multi-county metro area. 

 

Q: Will you offer all equipment for the HEEHRA program? 

A: We are currently planning to offer all allowable program improvements. 

 

Q: Where will you be receiving the equipment to be installed in the homes? Do you have a distributor for these materials? 

A: Qualified contractors for the program, will be responsible for receiving the products/goods to install. 

 

Q: For nonprofit managed multi-family buildings, how can we purchase new appliances through this program? 

A: Owners of multi-family buildings are able to apply for the programs.  

Eligibility, Eligible Uses, and Funding

click to expand

Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates (HEEHRA)

Home Efficiency Rebates (HOMES)
  • Early admin allocation - $2,500,000
  • Early admin allocation - $2,500,000
  • Rebates to provide single- and multi-family households with discounts for high-efficiency home appliances and equipment.
  • Rebates for energy efficiency retrofits range from $2,000-$4,000 for individual households and up to $400,000 for multifamily buildings.
  • Includes a $14,000 cap per household, with a $8,000 cap for heat pump costs, $1,750 for a heat pump water heater, and $4,000 for panel/service upgrade. Other eligible rebates include electric stoves and clothes dryers, and insulation/air sealing measures.
  • Up to $2,000 for retrofits reducing energy use by 20% or more, and up to $4,000 for retrofits saving 35% or more.

*States may increase the maximum amount available for low-income households upon approval from DOE. 

For more information on the rebate programs, visit DOE’s website here.

If you have any input that you would like to share with the State of Ohio as we design this rebate program, please email energysavings@development.ohio.gov with suggestions.
