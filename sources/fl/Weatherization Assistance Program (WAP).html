          Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)

          Share:

          The Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP) assists low-income homeowners with making their homes energy efficient through the installation of cost-saving measures, such as:

          • Attic insulation
          • Low-flow showerheads
          • Water heater jackets
          • Energy efficient light bulbs
          • Weather-stripping
          • Energy Recovery Ventilation (ERV), including bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans
          • Repair or replacement of exterior doors, windows, refrigerators, water heaters and cooling and heating systems

           

          • To apply:

            • Download and print the application  
            • Mail completed applications and supporting documentation to: Community Action and Human Services Department at 701 NW 1st Comi, 11 th Floor, Miami, Florida 33136.

            Please submit the following supporting documents with your completed application:

            1. Valid Florida driver's license or State ID card for household members 18 and over.
            2. Copy of Social Security Cards for every household member.
            3. Proof of income for the past twelfth month (last 6 paystubs, 2023 SSA or SSI award letter, 2023 pension/retirement statement and/or unemployment compensation).
            4. Most recent Florida Power & Light Company (FPL) utility bill.
            5. Proof of homeownership (Warranty Deed, Combined Tax Notice and/or Mmigage Statement).

            Please be advised services will be provided on a first come,first serve basis and/or the availability of funds.

            • Reside in Miami-Dade County
            • Be 18 years or older and possess a valid State of Florida ID
            • Be a US Citizen of have legal permanent resident status
            • Have a total household income that does not exceed 200% of the federal poverty guidelines
              • 1 member in household: $29,160
              • 2 members in household: $39,440
              • 3 members in household: $49,720
              • 4 members in household: $60,000
              • 5 members in household: $70,280
              • 6 members in household: $80,560
              • 7 members in household: $90,840
              • 8 members in household: $101,120
              • For each additional member in household with more than 8 people, add $10,280

            Sumbit the 

            • Have a valid Social Security Card for all household members
            • Provide proof of income for all household members for the past 12-month (last six paystubs, 2021 SSA or SSI award letter, 2021 pension or retirement statement or unemployment compensation) 
              • Current SNAP Benefits Printout
              • Current Veterans Benefits
              • Employer letter on company letterhead verifying employment start date, pay rate, frequency of pay and average hours worked per week
              • Most recent Florida Power & Light utility bill
            • Current Child Support Verification Printout
            • TANF Verification Printout
            • Current Worker Compensation Benefits
            • Notarized letter verifying the dollar amount of how much and how often, if someone is assisting you (attach income documentation such as pay stubs for the person assisting you)
            • Income Self-Declaration Form
            • Proof of homeownership (warranty deed, combined tax notice or mortgage statement)

            Updated 07/29/2024

          • Mechanical Measures:

            • Clean, tune, repair or replace heating and cooling systems
            • Install duct and heating pipe installation
            • Install programmable thermostats and other HVAC controls
            • Repair and replace water heaters
            • Install water heater tank insulation
            • Insulate water heating pipes
            • Install solar water heating systems
            • Install waste heat recovery devices

            Health and Safety Measures:

            • Complete combustion appliance safety testing
            • Repair or replace vent systems to ensure combustion gas draft safely outside
            • Install mechanical ventilation to ensure adequate indoor air quality
            • Assess fire hazards, install smoke and carbon monoxide alarms when needed
            • Evaluate mold and moisture hazards
            • Perform incidental safety repairs when needed

            Building Shell Measures:

            • Install wall, floor, ceiling, attic or foundation insulation
            • Complete blower door testing
            • Perform air sealing
            • Repair or replace primary windows and doors
            • Install storm windows and doors
            • Install window film or solar screen windows louvers and awnings
            • Repair minor roof and wall leaks prior to attic and wall insulation

            Electrical Baseload Measures: 

            • Install efficient light sources
            • Replace refrigerators and freezers with energy efficient models

          • Wind mitigation inspections and grant awards available

            • For more information visit My Safe FL Home
            • For inquiries email info@mysafeflhome.com
